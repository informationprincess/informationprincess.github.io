“A significant practical or service experience”

Baseline

Give back. As much as you have received help and support from others, give back for the greater good of the community. This is what my family taught me and it was made clear the more success I experienced, the greater my responsibility to help others succeed. Therefore in every position I find myself in, I use the skills and opportunities at my disposal to make things better.

The very nature of library work is to be of service connecting users with the information they need. Working in public libraries I not only learned some of the practical arts of librarianship but began to apply them to assist others. When a new subgenre of teen literature gained popularity, I volunteered to create a bibliography on the topic for library staff to utilize with patrons. During my elementary education studies, classmates and instructors relied on me for book recommendations and information about publishing trends. I was happy to oblige and loved being of service.

My Development

One of the things my husband and I love most about our children’s school is an emphasis on family involvement. In our gratitude for the well-rounded education our kids receive, we make an effort to support the school in the ways that we can. So when the school director approached me and asked if I’d take on a project involving their book collection, I gladly accepted. When she realized I was both working and going to school, she tried to take back the request, but I pointed out that this was a perfect opportunity to apply the skills I was learning in my masters program.

Spruce Street School is a Pre-K through 5th grade school of 82 students located in downtown Seattle. As a small school, they don’t have a librarian and had not had a formal library area. But they certainly have books. Over the years, hundreds of books had been purchased, donated or left to the school. Each teacher keeps a core collection in their classroom but the rest were stored in loose order in an unused room. The plan was to use the summer break period for moving the books into a room that would become the library and breakout study area.

My first priority was to find out what the school needed from the collection. I created and administered a survey to the teachers and staff to ascertain how the collection was being used. Based on that information, I established a policy for retention and began going through the books. This was an informative experience for me because I had to change my habitual way of thinking. The large public library system that I work for emphasizes maintaining a clean, up-to-date and attractive collection for all ages. The focus is entertainment and lifelong learning. At the school, I was retaining books to support the curricular and pleasure reading needs of 4 through 11 year olds while remembering that the budget for replacement books would be much smaller.

Because no educator ever wants to throw out a book, there were many items that were irrelevant or unusable. I filled up over 20 boxes of books which the school donated to local shelters. I made a list of subject areas that needed more titles and reported to the school staff and faculty. By the time school started, the usable books were on the shelves of the newly dubbed library. The unexpected benefit was the positive response of parents and students. At the first day of school festivities, several parents told me how happy they were to finally have a “real” library in the school. But the best response was when my seven year old told his class during a school tour, “That’s the library my mommy made!”

Additional Experiences

  • For over two years I have served as a member of Sno-Isle Libraries’ Reader’s Advisory Team. This team creates booklists and content for the library website and advises on training for public services staff.
  • Last year I was asked to coordinate a women’s book club for my church. For each month’s selection I gather background information and questions to share with the group and moderate the discussion.
  • For a school fundraiser, I donated my services as moderator for two book discussion parties and brought appetizers related to the stories for a complete book experience.
