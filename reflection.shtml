Reflections of the Journey

Looking far back into my life, it seems clear that I decided to climb this particular mountain at an early age. I fell in love with learning as soon as I understood the concept and had determined to achieve the highest educational level the world could offer. Of course, as they say, life happens. Although my immediate focus changed at times, it is now evident that my underlying plan, the desire of my heart, was never altered. Despite myself, I have made it to the pinnacle labeled “master’s degree”. From my new vantage point, I ask myself what has changed. Is it simply that I’m higher up in the world? Or has the journey up the mountain transformed me somehow?

The moment I was accepted to the MLIS program, I began to, and continue to, make the most of my opportunities. Throughout this portfolio I have demonstrated how these opportunities have shaped me intellectually and professionally. The overarching lessons I learned from my experiences fall into three categories: the power of passion, the power of self-knowledge and the power of endurance.

The Power of Passion

It was certainly my passions that brought me here: a passion for information, a passion for librarianship and a passion for knowledge. As I entered the program and discussed my passions with others, I found common ground upon which to build relationships. These relationships led to my most successful projects such as the iPeer program, reader’s advisory research and reorganizing a school library. I also realized that my passion for a subject is reflected in a higher quality for final product as exemplified by the training I conducted and the website I built. My experiences in the program have taught me the value of giving voice to my passions and collaborating with others to create meaningful work.

The Power of Self-Knowledge

I love the oft quoted line from Hamlet, “This above all: to thine own self be true”. Yet I feel the verity of this statement comes in the conclusion of the thought, “And it must follow, as the night the day, Thou cans't not be false to any man.” The more knowledge I have of my own intentions, habits and abilities, the more I can leverage that knowledge as I make commitments to other people and organizations. Through this program, I have gained a clearer picture of what I can accomplish, the personal strengths I can utilize and what work offers me the most satisfaction. Through trial and error I also discovered my personal limit on the number of projects and concerns I can maintain at a time. All this self-knowledge has made me a better employee, a better team member and a happier person overall.

The Power of Endurance

I occasionally ask myself why I can’t just sit still. But there’s so much I desire to accomplish and I know I have the ability to do so. These big dreams require big efforts. While in this program I came to appreciate how great achievements are built from consistent, sustained hard work. Whether a class assignment, a volunteer project or earning a master’s degree, I performed each step required to bring about success in the long term. When my progress stalled or seemed overwhelming, I drew upon resources both inside myself and within those around me. Discovering my capability to see a major achievement through to the end was one of the greatest rewards of this program.

This journey has indeed been transformative. As I move on to climb other mountains, I am wiser and better prepared to reach the top.
