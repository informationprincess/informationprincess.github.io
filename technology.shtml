“Significant project or product involving information technologies, in which you participated in its design and development”

Baseline

I still remember all the kids in my elementary school being shuffled into an unused classroom, grade by grade, to see the personal computers donated by Apple. The green screen and blinking cursor offered a new world to be explored. Always interested in trying something new, I found personal computing fun and useful at an early age.

One of my most memorable school assignments was a multimedia presentation I developed on the history of radio utilizing computer technology available at the time. During my undergraduate years I quickly grasped the power of email to connect with friends and share information. The rise of the Web suited my drive for information and connections. In the workplace, I embraced software tools that allowed me to instruct and inform in entertaining ways, such as developing PowerPoint presentations on library databases aimed at middle school students.

My Development

My first quarter in the program, students studying primarily via online tools were asked to take the course Internet Technologies and Applications. I knew I was a reasonably savvy Internet user and found most end-user applications intuitive to use for my purposes. Yet I was also aware how shallow my understanding was compared to the potential of what I could know about this technology I depend upon. This course taught me about the hardware and technologies that create my daily Internet experience. Having this knowledge added to my confidence in computing because I had more understanding of the processes behind it. I always prefer to know what’s happening behind the curtain when possible.

For me the heart of this class was learning basic skills in website creation and development. While my classmates commented that this was a “neat” or “useful” exercise, it was significantly more than that for me. By learning some simple codes, I had gained the power to create and share with the world instantaneously. The experience echoed the thrill of first realizing I could use pen and paper to communicate with others. It was a monumental shift from being a passive Web user to being an active Web builder. Immediately I set up a student homepage to share information with my friends and family about my school program.

We were encouraged to design our website final project around something we were interested in. I didn’t want my site to simply reflect a hobby of mine. I decided it should be something I’d want to come back to and build into a potentially useful site for others. I was inspired by one of my favorite sites, No Flying, No Tights, started by a library worker to share her reviews of graphic novels. Therefore I created Sounds Great as a place to begin sharing my experiences with audio books, an increasingly popular entertainment source. On my site I laid out eight basic categories representing the genres I tend to listen to and wrote an initial review for each one. I received positive feedback from my classmates and the librarians to whom I showed my new site. I was particularly pleased that I had “hand coded” the site in HTML and used a CSS for the layout instead of relying on editing software. I knew in the future I’d occasionally need the assistance of software to create a more complicated site (such as this one). But the experience had given me a sense of my own potential and inspired me for future projects.

Additional Experiences

  • I created a basic website for the iPeer program that I co-created and administrated for one year. The site included an intake form for program participants.
  • To support the reader’s advisory training I was planning, I created a blog on the topic. It became a place to gather links and information relevant to the training and its subject matter.
  • To further my knowledge of useful web design I took a 3 credit seminar in Information Architecture offered through the Masters of Science in Information Management Program.
  • Last year I was appointed a member of the Web Advisory Team for my employer's new website. In this capacity, I’ve had the fascinating opportunity to assist in usability testing of the site, discuss user behavior and consider better designs for user needs. I’ve also been responsible for the proofing various pages on our site.
