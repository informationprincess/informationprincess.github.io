Greetings!

Thank you for visiting the graduate portfolio of Sarah Evans. This site represents the culmination of my experiences while studying for my Masters of Library and Information Science. It captures but a slice of the rich intellectual and professional development I experienced while attending the Information School at the University of Washington. It is intended as evidence of the qualities and skills I have honed to ensure future success in the profession.


This portfolio is designed to show significant work in five essential areas; teaching or training, leadership, practical or service, sustained intellectual argument and information technologies. Each area is a separate page organized into three sections. The “baseline” section contains my general philosophy related to that area and my relevant experiences prior to beginning my master’s studies. “My development” details a specific experience during my graduate studies and how it shaped me intellectually and professionally.  “Additional experiences” are related work that contributed to my development in that particular area. Each area contains photographs and/or documents as supporting evidence of my development. You will find more selected evidence on the course studies page. In summary, I offer a reflection page that synthesizes the elements of my master’s program experience.


I hope you enjoy exploring the evidence of my intellectual and professional journey over the last three years. It has been a mountain worth climbing and I owe a debt of gratitude to those who’ve sustained and supported me so graciously.


Feel free to contact me with comments, questions or suggestions .
